The Math Survivor

A tragicomedy of numbers, procrastination, and extreme caffeine intake.

(Scroll down if you dare...)

↓

Chapter 1: The Untouched Artifact

For exactly 364 days, my Math book served a noble purpose.

It was a fantastic mousepad. It leveled my wobbly desk perfectly. Once, I even used it to kill a spider.

Did I open it? Absolutely not. I believed in learning through osmosis. I thought if I slept next to the book, the equations would naturally diffuse into my brain.

Chapter 2: The Night Before (9:00 PM)

The realization hit me like a truck full of geometry boxes.

"I have a Math paper tomorrow."

I finally opened the book. The table of contents looked like ancient Egyptian hieroglyphics. What is a "Calculus"? Is "Trigonometry" a type of dinosaur?

Caffeine Level in Bloodstream:

*Heart palpitations intensify*

Chapter 3: The 3:00 AM Delusions

At 3 AM, the panic faded and was replaced by pure, unadulterated delusion.

I convinced myself I didn't need to learn formulas. "I will simply derive them all from scratch in the exam hall," I whispered to my empty coffee mug.

I looked at the integration symbol (∫) and thought it was an elegant musical note. I was losing my mind.

Chapter 4: The Battlefield (9:00 AM)

The invigilator handed out the question paper. I took a deep breath. "I got this."

I looked at Question 1:
"Find X."

Me: "Bro, he left me. Let him go. Move on."

Question 2:
"If John has 50 watermelons and throws 12 at a train moving at 60km/h..."

Me: "John needs a therapist, not a mathematician."

Epilogue: The Walk of Shame

I left the exam hall after an hour. Not because I was finished, but because my brain had successfully uninstalled "Math.exe" to free up storage space.

When my friends asked "How was it?", I just smiled the smile of a broken person and said,

"I wrote my name perfectly."

RIP My GPA (August - May) 🪦
